Lake-Effect Snow

A deep dive into the meteorology behind Ontario's most intense snowfall events.

What is Lake-Effect Snow?

Lake-effect snow occurs when **cold, dry air masses move over the relatively warmer lake waters**. The warmth of the lake causes the air to pick up moisture, which then condenses and falls as snow.

Factors that affect lake-effect snow:

  • Temperature contrast between air and water
  • Wind direction and speed
  • Fetch (distance air travels over the lake)
  • Atmospheric instability

In Ontario, the Great Lakes play a major role in **producing heavy snow bands**, leading to **blizzard conditions** and significant accumulation in affected regions.
